Cardio Monitoring

Heart Rate Monitoring: Heart Rate (HR): The number of heartbeats per minute. Normal resting heart rate for adults is typically between 60 and 100 beats per minute.
Blood Pressure Monitoring: Blood Pressure (BP): The force of blood against the walls of the arteries. It is measured in millimeters of mercury (mmHg) and consists of two values - systolic (pressure during heartbeats) and diastolic (pressure between heartbeats). Electrocardiogram (ECG or EKG):
ECG: A test that records the electrical activity of the heart over a period of time. It is commonly used to diagnose heart conditions, such as arrhythmias and heart attacks.

Medical Treatment

Medical treatment refers to the use of medical interventions to address or manage a health condition, illness, or injury. It encompasses a wide range of approaches, including medications, surgical procedures, physical therapies, lifestyle modifications, and other interventions aimed at restoring or improving a person's health.
